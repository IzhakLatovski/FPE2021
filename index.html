Female Perpetrators Expo 2021

About

An Innovative project that looks at the unspoken aspect of The Holocaust - the female perpetrators - through a Travelling exhibition. It is time we acknowledge the females who were involved in the atrocities. The exhibit shows who these women were and what they did to be able to develop and create a deeper understanding of The Holocaust and its perpetrators. It also equalizes the gendered view of The Holocaust. The question of "Ordinary People" and the making of mass murderers is presented by shocking examples that provokes the visitors' thoughts.

The exhibit will be presented in Museums, memorial sites, educational and cultural centers, schools, universities, and colleges.

The exhibit itself is portable, easily maneuvered and easily built. The content will invent engagement of the audience.

Academics from different areas across the world are presenting their views in short clips.

The exhibition emphasizes educational aspects for various visitors.

It accompanied by an interactive App/Website to expand on select topics.

Our team

Nicole Sabag

Lives in Qiryat ata, Israel. Studying BA in Criminology at Western Galilee Collage. The Holocaust is very important to me as my great grandparents were veterans from World War Two and fought against the Nazis. I know they would like me to perpetuate the memory of what occurred and to make sure that the next generation understand what happened. I chose this project because I am interested to understand how people managed to commit such heinous crimes without previously being horrendous criminals.

Dr. Daniela Ozacky Stern

Lives in Ramot Menashe, Israel. Holds a PhD in Jewish History with a special focus on the Holocaust. Teaches on The Holocaust Studies Program at Western Galilee College in Akko, Israel. Currently conducting a post-doctorate at Bar Ilan University. "I was always interested in learning about The Holocaust and decided to devote myself to researching this topic and share it with my students. As a 'third-generation' Holocaust survivor it is my mission to continue exploring what happened during this horrible time".

Hadi Shchada

Lives in Kefar Yassif, Israel. Studying BA in Education at Western Galilee College. "I chose to learn about the Holocaust from when I studied it in high school, in Brazil. I continued my studies on the subject in College and received a distinction in my Holocaust studies. My goal is to expand my knowledge about this topic and share this valuable knowledge to the world. Every human being should know about what happened during the Holocaust".

Lauryn Winson

Lives in Carterton, Oxfordshire, United Kingdom. Studying BA Honours in History at The University of Winchester. My research focuses on the female perpetrators of The Holocaust, I feel it is necessary to acknowledge all the perpetrators that committed war crimes within The Holocaust, so we can understand as different societies that gender does not influence the way we treat others. The Holocaust as a topic has been widely researched to understand why people acted in the ways they did, yet the female involvement has been somewhat ignored and to be able to fully understand The Holocaust for further generations is to acknowledge all those involved.
